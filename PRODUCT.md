# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary: gallerists, curators, and collectors.** They arrive having heard the
name or followed a link, and within seconds they are deciding whether Emanuele
Longo is a serious artist worth an exhibition, a studio visit, or an acquisition.
Their context is professional and skeptical: they have seen a thousand artist
sites and most look amateur. The site must read, immediately, as
"exhibition-ready."

**Secondary: any visitor who encounters the work.** Press, students, fellow
artists, the curious. For them the job is emotional, not transactional: to be
pulled into the imagery and feel the symbolic, gothic world the paintings inhabit.

The job to be done, across both: take Emanuele Longo seriously, and feel the work
before reading a word about it.

## Product Purpose

A bilingual (Italian / English) portfolio for the painter Emanuele Longo, whose
practice he names "Espressionismo visionario" (Visionary Expressionism) — mixed
media on cardboard and canvas, dense symbolist imagery, archetypal and spectral.
The site presents three bodies of work (dipinti / paintings, disegni / drawings,
opere digitali / digital works), his biography, and his artist statement.

Success is a curator who finishes the visit convinced the work merits real
attention, and a general visitor who leaves having felt something. The interface
is not a container for the art; on a portfolio, the interface IS the work's
first impression. It must carry the gravitas of a printed monograph and the
immersion of a darkened gallery room.

## Brand Personality

**Visceral. Spectral. Archetypal.**

Voice and tone draw from the artist's own manifesto: art "born of blood and fire,"
a "descent into the psychic underworld," symbols "in perennial transmutation."
The interface should feel like the slow turning of pages in an exhibition
catalog AND like stepping into a dark room where the paintings glow off the wall.
Restrained chrome, expansive imagery, deliberate pacing. Serious, never sterile.
Atmospheric, never gimmicky. The drama belongs to the work; the site sets the
stage and then gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / template.** No centered hero with a tagline and two buttons,
  no feature-card grids, no rounded-everything "AI app" look, no gradient CTAs.
  This is art, not a product launch.
- **Sterile white-cube minimalism.** Not the default Squarespace art portfolio:
  stark white background, tiny thumbnails in a rigid uniform grid, zero
  atmosphere. Too cold and too generic for work this dark and dense.
- **Instagram-feed flatness.** No endless scroll of equal-weight images with no
  curation, hierarchy, or narrative. Every body of work is curated, not dumped.
- **Loud, effect-for-its-own-sake awwwards.** Motion and atmosphere must serve
  the imagery, never upstage it. No gratuitous spectacle.

## Design Principles

1. **The work speaks first.** Imagery leads every screen; words support, never
   precede. A visitor should feel the paintings before reading about them.
2. **Catalog gravitas, gallery immersion.** Hold the tension between an editorial
   art-book (serif voice, generous margins, considered pacing) and a cinematic
   dark room (imagery filling the frame, slow atmospheric motion). Both, not one.
3. **Curate, never dump.** 155 works across three bodies. Hierarchy, sequence,
   and breathing room everywhere; never an undifferentiated wall of thumbnails.
4. **Restraint earns the drama.** The chrome is quiet so the art can be loud.
   Motion is deliberate and slow; when something moves, it means something.
5. **Bilingual without compromise.** Italian is the authoritative voice (the
   manifesto's force lives in it); English serves the international audience.
   Neither feels like an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Stance: **AA where it counts, atmosphere first.** Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on
all body text, navigation, and interactive controls. Allow lower-contrast,
atmospheric or decorative type for mood where it does not carry essential
information. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support (no entrance animations,
parallax, or continuous motion when requested — instant state changes only).
Keyboard-navigable throughout. Correct `lang` attributes per locale (it / en).
Meaningful `alt` text on every artwork drawn from its title and metadata.
